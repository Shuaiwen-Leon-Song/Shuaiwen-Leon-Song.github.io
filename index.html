Bio

I am currently a Senior Lecturer (Associate Professor in North America) at the School of Computer Science of University of Sydney. I am also an Affiliated Professor with University of Washington Electrical Engineering department. Prior to my appointment at Sydney, I worked for U.S. Department of Energy Lab for five years as senior research scientist and technical lead. My research interests are software-hardware co-design (holistic system design), high performance computing, future accelerator-driven system design for AI and planet-scale virtual reality. The special feature of my research methodology is to create holistic cross-stack system design via a cross-layer approach combining architecture research, runtime system and compiler support, along with emerging workloads analysis. I am 2017 IEEE Technical Consortium HPC early career award winner, a Lawrence scholar, an recipient of Paul E. Torgersen Excellent research award, 2015 and 2017 DOE PNNL lab outstanding performance award, two Super Computing (SC) best paper runner-ups (2015 and 2017), and 2017 HiPEAC paper award.

I have published in the major HPC and computer architecture conferences including ISCA, HPCA, ASPLOS, MICRO, and Supercomputing. I have served as organizing committee or PC member for several major system venues including ASPLOS, SC, ICS, IPDPS and HPDC. My past research has been supported by U.S. government agencies including DOE o.ce of science (ASCR), DoD, DARPA and Lab LDRD. During my tenure at PNNL, I led two DOE lab LDRD projects on AI-driven architecture design and large-scale data analytics acceleration.
